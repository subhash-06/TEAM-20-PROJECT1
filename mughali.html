MUGHLAI BIRYANI


INGREDIENTS

BASMATI

1 KG

CURD

1/2 CUP

WATER

2 LITRES

GINGER GARLIC

1 tbsp

GREEN CHILIES

9

CILANTRO

2 ROUGHLY CHOPPED

OIL

2 tbsp

ONIONS

2-ROUGHLY CHOPPED

GHEE

1/4 CUP

CHICKEN

4 CHICKEN BREASTS

BIRYANI SPICES

EACH-2

STEPS

(60 MINUTES)

  • Medium cut the chicken breasts and take it in a bowl and add 1/2 curd,1/2 spoon ginger garlic paste,2 cilantro,4 strighly chopped chillies,1/2 spoon Garam masala,1/2 spoon salt,biryani spices each-1 mix the whole ingrediants and add 1/4 cup oil,1/2 spoon ghee,1/4 cup frid onions and mix again.(This part is called marination)
  • Keep the bowl in Refrigrirator for 30mins.
  • In the mean while take 2 cups of water in stockpot add biryani spice each-2,1/ spoon salt,5 straighly chopped chillies,1/2 spoon ginger garlic paste and boil it upto water turn too hot.
  • After boiling add soaked basmati rice and lemon juice to the stockpot.
  • After cooking of 60% of basmati rice remove water from stockpot.
  • Add chicken to the stockpot layer by layer and allow it to cook for 2mins.
  • After that add ghee to the edges of stockpot and cilantro to it and allow it to cook for another 2 mins.
  • Run maida dough around the stockpot and close the lid.
  • Cook it for 8min on high flame or 7min on low flame.
  • After 8mins rest it for 20mins.
  • After 20mins mix the rice and turn of the stove.
  • Then add fresh cilantro leaves.
  • Serve Hot

Mughalai biryani is a rich and aromatic rice dish that originated in the Mughal era of India. It is made with fragrant basmati rice, tender meat (usually chicken or mutton), and a blend of spices. The biryani is cooked in layers, allowing the flavors to meld together and create a delicious and satisfying meal.

Indian recipes are said to be historic treasures that tells the historic tales of our ancestors. This is why we are trying to save them, in order to safeguard our history from dying down.
